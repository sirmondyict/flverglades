fighting invasive species in the everglades

the florida everglades is one of the most unique wetlands on earth.
today, invasive species are spreading rapidly and threatening
the plants, animals, and balance of this fragile ecosystem.

what are invasive species?

species introduced to an environment where they do not naturally belong.

without natural predators, they spread quickly and disrupt the balance of native ecosystems.

why the everglades is at risk

invasive species are changing the food chain, destroying habitats, and pushing native wildlife toward extinction.

rapid population growth

many invasive animals reproduce quickly and outcompete native species for food and space.

habitat destruction

invasive plants and predators damage wetlands and remove shelter for native wildlife.

food web disruption

when key species disappear, the entire ecosystem becomes unstable and harder to recover.

invasive species in the everglades

burmese python

a powerful predator responsible for major declines in mammal populations.

lionfish

a fast-spreading fish that consumes native reef species at alarming rates.

australian pine

an invasive tree that crowds out native plants and damages wetland habitats.

how people are helping

scientists, volunteers, and communities are working together to restore the everglades.

research and monitoring

tracking invasive populations to understand how they spread and how to stop them.

removal programs

organized efforts remove invasive animals and plants from sensitive habitats.

public awareness

educating communities to prevent releasing non-native pets and plants.

be part of the solution


small actions can make a big difference in protecting the everglades for future generations.
