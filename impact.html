the real impact of invasive species

biodiversity collapse

invasive species compete with native animals for food and habitat, often leading to dramatic population declines. in the everglades, studies have shown that some mammal populations have dropped by over 90%.

when native species disappear, the ecosystem loses balance, making it harder for other organisms to survive.

ecosystem imbalance

ecosystems rely on a delicate balance between predators, prey, and plant life. invasive species disrupt this balance by over-predating or outcompeting native organisms.

this creates a chain reaction where multiple species are affected, not just the ones directly targeted.

long-term environmental damage

invasive plants and animals physically change the environment. they can alter soil composition, water flow, and habitat structure.

over time, these changes make it extremely difficult for native ecosystems to recover, even if the invasive species are removed.
